ABOUT

Presently, Celso Isidro is a full stack JavaScript Programming Student at ComIT, Canada. In the past 9 years, he devoted himself in Remote sensing and Geographic Information System (GIS) Technologies application to mining operations and environmental management. He concentrates on satellite and LiDAR imaging of land use and land cover changes, spatial modelling of inland water qualities, topographic and mine exploration drilling surveys and monitoring of mine rehabilitation.

Moreover, Celso was involved in international projects such as the following: (a) Philippine Government Project (CLINN-GEM) that deployed the first green technology in the country as an alternative to gold amalgamation and cyanidation processes in small-scale mining regions; and (b) An Australian-funded Program that was concentrated on the detection of diffuse, dynamic and often remote activities of mining activities using Object-based Image Analysis (OBIA) and their corresponding contribution to inland water quality.
